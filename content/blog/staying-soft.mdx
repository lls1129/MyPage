It's easy, in a year that asks a lot of you, to start filing the edges off
yourself — to mistake hardness for strength and quietness for peace. I keep
having to remind myself that *soft* is not the same as *fragile*.

## the smallest practice

The thing that helps me most isn't a system. It's a list of small,
unremarkable things I keep doing on purpose:

- write down one nice thing someone did, before I forget
- water the basil even when it looks fine
- text back, even if it's a week late
- look at the moon, on purpose, at least once a week

None of those are coping mechanisms. They're closer to little promises I
make to the version of myself who tends this corner of the internet.

## what staying soft costs

I won't pretend it's free. Staying open means staying tender. Some weeks
that tenderness gets bumped into and it bruises and I want to climb inside
a shell for a while. So I do — for an evening, sometimes a weekend.

> Rest is not the opposite of softness. It's how softness lasts.

Then I come back out and water the basil again.

## a tiny manifesto

If you're reading this and your year has been a lot too: you are allowed to
be both careful and warm. You are allowed to protect yourself without
becoming guarded. You are allowed to keep the parts of yourself that the
world doesn't always reward.

I think the world needs them, actually. ✿
